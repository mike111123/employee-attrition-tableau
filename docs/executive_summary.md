# Executive Summary — Employee Attrition Analytics Dashboard

**Analyst:** Mike Redding
**Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics (1,470 employees, 35 fields)
**Tool:** Tableau Public
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Overview

This analysis examines employee attrition patterns across a 1,470-person IBM workforce dataset to identify the highest-risk segments and recommend targeted retention strategies. Three key findings emerged from the dashboard analysis.

---

## Finding 1 — Sales Has the Highest Attrition Volume

The Sales department accounts for the largest share of attrition in absolute numbers, driven primarily by Sales Representatives (not Sales Executives). R&D follows in volume but has a lower rate relative to headcount. HR has the smallest department but a disproportionately high attrition rate.

**Recommendation:** Focus retention investment on Sales Representatives first, HR roles second.

---

## Finding 2 — Employees Leave in the First 2 Years

Attrition drops sharply after the 2-year mark across all departments.

| Tenure Band | Attrition Rate |
|---|---|
| 0-1 year | ~31% |
| 1-3 years | ~24% |
| 3-5 years | ~14% |
| 5+ years | ~8% |

**Recommendation:** Implement a structured 90-day and 1-year check-in program to catch at-risk employees before the 12-month mark.

---

## Finding 3 — Highest-Risk Cohort Identified

**Sales Representatives + Under 2 years tenure + Monthly income below $3,000 = 52% attrition rate**

This specific cohort is identifiable, targetable, and addressable. At 52%, roughly 1 in 2 employees in this group leaves the company.

**Recommendation:** Conduct a compensation review for Sales Representatives earning below $3,000/month. A modest salary adjustment or performance bonus structure could significantly reduce attrition.

---

## Business Impact Estimate

Assuming an average replacement cost of 50% of annual salary ($18,000 for a $36,000/yr Sales Rep), reducing this cohort's attrition rate from 52% to 30% across 50 employees would save an estimated **$198,000 annually** in turnover costs.
